Wastage of water during HOLI

Holi and Water wastage
A growing concern

Every year, thousands of liters of water are wasted during Holi celebrations. Water balloons, buckets, and water cannons add to the fun, but they also deplete one of our most precious resources—clean water.


On average, a single person uses 200-500 liters of water during Holi

In urban areas, Holi festivities contribute to a 30% spike in water usage

Over 40% of India’s population faces water scarcity issues

Importance of water conservation

Water is a finite resource. With climate change and growing populations, water shortages are becoming more common across India and the world. Wasting water during Holi, a single day, contributes to long-term damage.

Water-free ways to celebrate HOLI


Play with dry powder colors that don’t require water for fun.

Celebrate with vibrant flower petals instead of water balloons.

Organize water-free Holi events like music festivals, dance performances, or cooking competitions.
Our platform promotes water conservation by encouraging eco-friendly celebrations during Holi. While the festival is a time of joy, excessive water use harms the environment and depletes resources. We aim to raise awareness about sustainable practices, offering tips for enjoying a dry, vibrant Holi and supporting initiatives that promote responsible water use. Together, we can celebrate mindfully and protect our precious water resources for a better future.
